Using the CNC Router!

For week 2, our project involved learning to operate the FabLab's Shaper handheld CNC router to create a personalized desktop organizer from a 12.5in x 8.5in block of birch. I designed my organizer using a free CAD software called Fusion 360. After designing and getting machine training from my professor, I went to work!

Creating a Design

I started off with the traditional pad and paper to sketch out ideas for my initial design. I say "Initial" because, after hearing how long large pocket cuts take, I had to change my design. My plan was to include a slot for my sticky note pad, my mouse, 4 sticky note tabs, and a required 3-inch circle. After taking some measurements of my objects, I began a sketch in Fusion 360. I added dimensions based on my measurements and spent a lot of time ensuring that the objects would actually fit in their designated spot.
Next, I "extruded" the design, giving it three-dimensions and specifying the depth of each pocket. This also allowed me to render and see what my final product would look like. After creating this design, I had training with Madison, and learned about the different kinds of cuts the shaper is capable of.

  • Pocket Cut: remove all material inside of the guide line
  • Interior Cut: cut inside the guide line
  • Exterior Cut: cut outside the guide line - this maintains dimensions
  • On-line Cut: cut along the guide line
